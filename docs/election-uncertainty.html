Visualizing Uncertainty of Partial U.S. Election Returns Close

In the summer of 2020 it became clear that early U.S. election returns — vote tallies in the first few hours after polls closed — would likely differ substantially from final vote count. Most news organizations adjusted some election reporting to account for this uncertainty. But most of their accompanying visualizations hardly changed from previous years.

Shortly after the election I pulled election return data and made a number of visualizations to illustrate the challenge and explore potential approaches. This work led to a collaboration with political scientist Alexander Coppock on a project to test the impact of different map-based election results visualizations.

While the research is ongoing, we have completed most of the maps for our study.

Languages and Tools: Observable, d3, topojson
